Pass-Through Embodiment (PTE)

The Future of Virtual Self-Representation

A Project by Kristoffer Waldow

Supervised by Arnulph Fuhrmann (TH Köln) & Daniel Roth (TU Munich)

Scientific Context & Methodology

Research Motivation: Current avatar-based embodiment approaches in Virtual Reality often suffer from tracking latency, morphological mismatches, and the "Uncanny Valley" effect, which can significantly diminish the user's sense of self-identification and presence.

Methodology: Pass-Through Embodiment (PTE) leverages real-time depth segmentation and stereoscopic video pass-through (e.g., via Meta Quest 3) to project a high-fidelity, photon-based representation of the user’s physical body directly into the virtual environment. This approach circumvents the need for complex skeletal tracking models.

Objective: This research aims to psychophysically validate the efficacy of real-body integration in immersive simulations, establishing a perceptual continuum between physical reality and virtual embodiment to enhance immersion and interaction fidelity.

Research Items

1. Validation

IEEE VR 2025 (TVCG)

A comparative study against customizable graphical avatars in a dynamic sorting task. Results: PTE demonstrated statistically significant improvements in General Presence and Body Ownership, confirming the hypothesis that PTE improves presence and embodiment compared to traditional customizable avatars.

Read Paper (TVCG)

2. Optimization

IEEE VR 2026 (TVCG)

Investigating boundary artifacts caused by low-resolution depth sensors. Innovation: A perceptual dithering algorithm using ordered noise (Bayer Matrix) to address the "Jagged Edge" problem. Finding: Dithering provides a superior perceptual quality over alpha blending while maintaining high performance.

3. Experience

IEEE VR 2026 (Demo)

A hands-on demonstration across diverse environments (Library, Urban, Indoor). Contribution: Validated that scene complexity and lighting conditions significantly influence the perceptual integration of the pass-through body, proving robust object interaction capabilities.

System Demonstration

Technical Architecture

The system integrates the Meta Depth API for robust occlusion management and real-time segmentation. A custom shader pipeline orchestrates the stereoscopic composition to minimize photon-to-photon latency.

Dithering Implementation: To resolve depth-boundary discontinuities ("jagged edges"), we employ an ordered dithering algorithm based on a Bayer Matrix. This approach creates a pseudo-transparency effect that mitigates the "ghosting" artifacts typical of alpha blending, while maintaining the computational efficiency required for mobile VR platforms.

Implications for Mixed Reality

Pass-Through Embodiment suggests a paradigm shift: moving from simulating the user to integrating the user.

Photon-Captured Integration

Grounding the virtual experience in the user's physical morphology yields significantly higher General Presence & Body Ownership than avatar reconstruction.

The Edge as Anchor

The boundary between realities is psychological. Dithered Edges provide a solid "grainy" anchor for agency, avoiding the "ghostly" artifacts of transparency.

Streamlined Immersion

Achieving high-fidelity embodiment without complex rigging or skinning. Leveraging native depth sensors offers stability with negligible GPU cost.

Rigorous Evaluation

Using the "Real-Mirror Metaphor" (aligning physical mirrors with virtual cutouts) forces a direct confrontation with self-representation artifacts.

Contextual Dependency

Integration quality depends on scene complexity and lighting. Photometric alignment is critical for achieving total visual coherence.

Scalable Social Presence

PTE offers a pathway for high-fidelity Telepresence. It resolves the presence-fidelity trade-off by transmitting the user's real appearance rather than a synthetic approximation.
